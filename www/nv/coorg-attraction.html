Talacauvery

46 kilometers from Madikeri lies Talacauvery, the source of the sacred river Cauvery. Climb up to a panoramic view of mountains and the beautiful shrine with a silver Shiva Linga which marks the spring from which the mighty Cauvery River flows. If you are visiting around October 17th you can experience Tula Sankramana day. Thousands gather here each year to witness the magical sudden spout of water from the spring. Enjoy a trek on the Brahmagiri peak which stands near the temple.

Bagamandala

Situated 39km from Madikeri, this is a sacred place frequented by many pilgrims who come to visit the famous Temples nearby. This place marks the sacred confluence or triveni sangama of three rivers –Cauvery, Kannike and Sujyoti. The Bagandeshwara Temple stands tall with Red tiled roofs overlooking an open courtyard, it is built in Karavali style. During 1785 to 1790, the area was occupied by the great King Tipu Sultan. In addition to its sacred personality it has historical significance which can be learnt from the inscriptions on stone in the temple.

Igguthappa

Near Bagamandala lies the Padi and Aiyangeri Temples. The Padi Igguthappa temple is considered one of the most important temples by the Kodavas. It holds the deity Iguthappa also known as Lord Subramanya.

Abbey Falls:

The picturesque Abbey Falls lies 9 km from Madikeri. In the middle of lush green coffee estate and cardamom plantations, the cool waters of a mountain stream gush energetically over a rock, perfect for a family picnic.
Omkareshwara Temple

About a kilometer away from the Madikeri main bus stand lies the Temple of Omkareshwara built by Lingarajendra II in 1820. The temple is built in Indo-Saracenic style which combines Kerala red tile sloping roofs and Islamic domes.
Raja’s Tomb:

Close to the Omkareshwara Temple lies the tombs of the kings that built the temple and ruled the area. Identical tombs of Veerarajendra ,Lingarajendra, and their wives stand together at this tourist spot.
Raja Seat:

A beautiful park with panoramic views of the mountains. The sunset is majestic to watch from here. Legend has it that the Kodava kings and their friends spent evenings at this park watching the orange sun disappear into the mountains.

The Fort:

It lies about 500 meters from the Madikeri main bus stand. Built by Mudduraja in 1681, its earthen structure was rebuilt by Tipu Sultan during his rule.
Dubare:

The Elephant camp at Dubare is a center for study of Elephant behavior. Across the river by boat takes you to the gentle giants camp, they go about their day with their mahouts bathing and eating. Besides elephant rides visitors can interact and feed and bath the elephants. If you’re lucky you can meet a young friendly baby elephant too.
Nisargadama:

A tranquil forest resort on the bank of the Cauvery River is a 36 km drive from Madikeri. The resort lies on a 64 acre island with lush forest and bamboo plants, enjoy the sights of Deer, Rabbits, Peacocks and Elephants. Take a boat ride or rent one of the resorts river side cottages.
Bylakuppe:

34 km from Madikeri lies Bylakuppe the largest Tibetian settlement in India. Around the glorious Temple sits a monastery, gompa of Sera Je and Sera Mey, Namdroling and Tashib lhumpo renowned as the seat of Panchen Lama. You can soak in the peaceful Tibetian culture as well as shop in the popular market outside. .
Mallalli Falls:

In the foothills of Pushpagiri lies the Mallalli Falls. Water, greenery ad breathtaking views surround you.

Honnamana Kere:

Situated in Somwarpet, Honnamama Kere Lake is the biggest in Coorg. It is a sacred lake named after the Goddess Honamana. The Pandavas are believed to have roamed this area and built caves in the mountains. Explore the mountains, cliffs and coffee plantations, go fishing in the lake or take a boat ride.

Chelavara Falls:

Another beautiful waterfall Coorg has to offer, flowing amidst lush greenery situated near Cheyyandane.

Thadiyandamol:

One of the best places for trekking in is Thadiyandamol peak, the highest mountain in Coorg. Whether you like a nature walk or an adventurous climb, the mountain has steep rugged climbing spots and paths as well. The famous Nalnad Palace is located at the base of Thadiyandamol.

Nalnad Palace:

The Nalnad Palace at the base of Thandiyandamol peak was built by Dodda Viraraja in 1792. History tells us that this palace was built after Doddavirarajendra escaped from Tipu Sultan’s troops. The walls and roof of this two level palace are adorned with paintings and art.
Nagarahole National Park (NNP):

Catch a glimpse of an elusive Tiger in the enchanting 247 square-mile National park in Karnataka. Besides Tigers, Nagarahole has an abundance of wildlife like Leopards, Wild Elephant and Gaur. The park is also shared by the Chital Spotted Deer, Mouse Deer, Four-horned Antelope, Wild Boar, Sloth Bear, Hyena, Mongoose, Civet, Otter, and more.
Irupu Falls:

Located on the highway to the Nagarahole Park are the stunning Irupu Falls. They flow from the Lakshmana Tirtha River which is a tributary of the great Cauvery River. The Shiva Temple is located on the bank of the Lakshmana Tirthi River, pilgrims come from all over to bath in the sacred waters.
 
Mandalpatti

22 km from Madikeri lies vast open grasslands on a small green mountain range. The grasslands on the hills are a beautiful sight.
